✖️ Multiply fractions

Multiply the tops together. Multiply the bottoms together. That's the whole rule.

\(\dfrac{1}{2} \times \dfrac{1}{3} = \dfrac{1 \times 1}{2 \times 3} = \dfrac{1}{6}\)
🧮 Fraction of a quantity

'Of' means multiply. Split the quantity into equal groups using the bottom number, then take as many groups as the top number says.

\(\dfrac{3}{4} \text{ of } 20 = \dfrac{3 \times 20}{4} = 15\)
🔁 Fraction of the rest

When the chain says 'of the rest,' multiply the leftover fractions together — don't add the bites.

\(\dfrac{1}{4} \text{ then } \dfrac{1}{3} \text{ of rest} \Rightarrow \dfrac{3}{4} \times \dfrac{2}{3} = \dfrac{1}{2} \text{ left}\)
➗ Divide fractions

Flip the divisor (the second fraction), then multiply. Never flip the first.

\(\dfrac{3}{4} \div \dfrac{1}{4} = \dfrac{3}{4} \times \dfrac{4}{1} = 3\)
