📖 Read a fraction

The bottom number tells you how many equal parts the whole is split into. The top number tells you how many of those parts you have.

\(\dfrac{3}{5}\)
👥 Fractions in a group

A fraction can describe part of a group, not just part of one shape. Bottom = total members of the group; top = count of the part you're naming.

\(\dfrac{3}{8} \text{ of the dots are filled}\)
✂️ Build equivalents

Multiply top AND bottom by the same number — the value stays the same, only the name changes.

\(\dfrac{1}{2} = \dfrac{1\times 3}{2\times 3} = \dfrac{3}{6}\)
⚖️ Compare fractions

Rewrite with a common denominator, then compare the tops. Or use benchmarks: is it near 0, near 1/2, or near 1?

\(\dfrac{2}{3} \text{ vs } \dfrac{3}{5}: \ \dfrac{10}{15} > \dfrac{9}{15}\)
🎯 Fractions describe quantities

A fraction isn't just a number — it's a description of a part compared to a whole. Always ask: what IS the whole here?

\(\dfrac{2}{3} \text{ of a pizza} \neq \dfrac{2}{3} \text{ of a class}\)
